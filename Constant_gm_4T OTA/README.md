# Constant-gm Biasing of a 4-Transistor OTA Using Negative Feedback

## Overview

This project explores the implementation of a constant-gm biasing architecture using a compact 4-transistor Operational Transconductance Amplifier (OTA) and a negative feedback loop in Cadence Virtuoso.

The objective was to study how feedback can be used to regulate the transconductance of an OTA and reduce its dependence on temperature and bias variations.

---

## Project Description

The design consists of a replica 4T OTA, an error amplifier, and a feedback network that generates the bias conditions for the main amplifier. The feedback loop continuously adjusts the common-mode voltage based on the current generated by the replica OTA.

The complete circuit was designed and simulated using gpdk180 CMOS technology with a 1.8 V supply.

| Parameter          | Value                         |

| Temperature Range  | -20°C to 100°C                |
| Design Environment | Cadence Virtuoso              |

---

## Work Done

* Designed and simulated a 4-Transistor OTA.
* Implemented a replica OTA for transconductance sensing.
* Developed a negative-feedback-based bias stabilization scheme.
* Performed transistor-level operating-point analysis.
* Verified bias conditions of the OTA and amplifier blocks.
* Conducted temperature sweep simulations to study transconductance variation.

---

## Results

The implemented architecture successfully demonstrated the operation of a feedback-based constant-gm biasing scheme.

Operating-point simulations confirmed proper biasing of the OTA and amplifier blocks. Temperature sweep analysis was performed over a wide temperature range to evaluate transconductance stability and study the effectiveness of the feedback loop.

Although the target specification of less than 1% transconductance variation was not achieved, the simulations provided valuable insight into the practical implementation and limitations of constant-gm bias circuits.

---

## Conclusion

This project provided practical experience in transistor-level analog circuit design, OTA implementation, and feedback-based bias stabilization. Through the design and simulation of a constant-gm architecture, the behavior of replica biasing techniques and temperature-dependent transconductance variation was studied. The project successfully demonstrated the overall operating principle of constant-gm bias generation and highlighted important design considerations for future optimization.
